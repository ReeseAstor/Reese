About the Author

A life dedicated to the written word

Reese Astor has been captivated by the power of stories since childhood, spending countless hours in the corners of libraries, lost in worlds that existed between the covers of well-worn books. This early love affair with literature blossomed into a lifelong passion for not just reading stories, but crafting them.

After earning a degree in English Literature, Reese spent several years working in publishing before making the leap to full-time writing. That decision, made over a cup of lukewarm coffee in a cramped apartment, turned out to be the first chapter of an extraordinary journey.

Reese's writing is characterized by lyrical prose, deeply nuanced characters, and narratives that explore the quiet complexities of human relationships. Each novel is a meditation on the themes that define our shared experience: love, loss, identity, and the eternal search for belonging.

When not writing, Reese can be found wandering through botanical gardens, collecting vintage typewriters (a slightly impractical but deeply satisfying hobby), or experimenting with recipes that rarely turn out as planned. Home is a cozy house filled with too many books and just the right amount of houseplants.

"Every story I write begins with a question I cannot answer and ends with the hope that someone, somewhere, will find a piece of themselves in the words I leave behind."

Reese's debut novel received critical acclaim and was longlisted for several prestigious awards. Since then, each subsequent work has further cemented a reputation for storytelling that is both intellectually stimulating and emotionally resonant.
